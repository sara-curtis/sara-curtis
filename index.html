I am a PhD student in the Department of Mathematics at University College London, working on the mathematics of microswimmer locomotion in viscous fluids.

My research combines low Reynolds number hydrodynamics, multiscale asymptotic analysis, and singularity methods to study how swimmers behave in confined or boundary-influenced environments.

Before my PhD, I completed an undergraduate degree in mathematics. Outside of research, I am interested in scientific computing and mathematical modelling.

Research

I
Microswimmer locomotion near boundaries
Using singularity methods and far-field approximations to understand how boundaries alter swimmer trajectories, drift velocities, and effective diffusion in viscosity-dominated environments.
II
Multi-timescale asymptotic analysis
Developing matched asymptotic expansions for swimmers whose internal dynamics evolve on timescales well-separated from their translational motion, enabling systematic reduction of complex models.
III
Low Reynolds number hydrodynamics
Studying Stokes flow in geometrically confined settings, including the role of rotational and straining components of background flows in shaping swimmer behaviour.
